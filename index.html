About

I'm a senior software engineer at Center Stage, a ticket-resale company, where I build the systems that manage inventory at scale — and the monitoring platforms that find new angles on what to buy and where to buy it.

The interesting part of that job is turning noisy, rate-limited marketplace data into reliable, low-latency signal. It's the same problem I've been drawn to for years: my own Ticket Monitor platform sustains 100M+ requests a day, and my eBay alerting product, eSnipe, serves 90+ active users in production.

I work AI-first. Claude, Cursor, Codex, and ChatGPT are part of my daily loop — scaffolding services, generating tests, pressure-testing designs, reviewing diffs. The leverage buys speed; the discipline keeps quality and testability non-negotiable. Fast and well-built stop being trade-offs when the workflow is designed for both.

Before Center Stage I spent three years at Fidelity leading internal deployment-platform work, acting as de facto technical lead through team transitions, and earning a reputation as the engineer you pull into design discussions. I hold an M.S. in Applied Computer Science (AI & ML concentration, 4.0 GPA) from Wentworth — and when I'm away from a terminal, I shoot portrait photography.
